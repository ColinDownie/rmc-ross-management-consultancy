The Ross Six‑Stage View

A clear, structured way of understanding organisations and making decisions that hold up in the real world.

1. Sequence

We establish the correct order of operations. Clarity begins with knowing what must happen first.

2. Selection

We identify the critical few. Not everything matters — we focus on what moves the system.

3. Iteration

We refine through controlled cycles. Progress is built through structured repetition, not guesswork.

4. Explanation

We make thinking visible. Clear explanation aligns teams and exposes assumptions early.

5. Implication

We map the consequences. Every decision has a shadow — we surface it before committing.

6. Application

We turn clarity into delivery. Structure, discipline and realism drive movement and results.
