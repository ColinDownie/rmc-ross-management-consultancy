Case Studies

Real organisations. Real structural change. Real leadership transformation.

Overview

Ross Management Consultancy works with organisations at moments of transition: growth, restructuring, leadership change, or operational friction. The case studies below illustrate how structural clarity, leadership alignment, and decision‑making frameworks transform performance.

Case Study 1: Scaling Beyond the Founder

Sector: Technology

Challenge: A founder‑led organisation had grown to 45 people, but all decisions still flowed through one individual. Bottlenecks were slowing delivery, leadership was unclear, and teams were compensating with heroic effort.

What RMC Did

  • Redesigned the organisational structure around clear accountability.
  • Established a leadership team with defined decision rights.
  • Implemented a leadership operating system with weekly, monthly, and quarterly rhythms.
  • Supported the founder through a transition to strategic leadership.

Outcome

Decision‑making decentralised, delivery speed increased, and the founder regained strategic bandwidth. The organisation scaled to 70+ people without structural strain.

Case Study 2: Leadership Team Alignment

Sector: Professional Services

Challenge: A high‑performing leadership team was operating in silos. Meetings were unproductive, decisions were inconsistent, and accountability was unclear.

What RMC Did

  • Conducted a leadership behaviour and decision‑flow diagnostic.
  • Redesigned the leadership team structure and clarified ownership.
  • Introduced a unified decision‑making framework.
  • Facilitated alignment sessions to embed shared behaviours.

Outcome

The leadership team began operating as a cohesive unit. Meetings became shorter and more effective, and decisions were made consistently across the organisation.

Case Study 3: Governance & Board Clarity

Sector: Non‑Profit

Challenge: The board and executive team were misaligned on roles, responsibilities, and decision boundaries. This created confusion, delays, and tension.

What RMC Did

  • Mapped decision rights between board and executive.
  • Redesigned governance structures and reporting lines.
  • Implemented a board operating rhythm with clear expectations.
  • Aligned leadership behaviours to support the new structure.

Outcome

Board‑executive friction reduced dramatically. Decisions became faster, reporting became clearer, and the organisation regained strategic momentum.
